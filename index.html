Department of Wildlife Sri Lanka

The Department of Wildlife Conservation has been entrusted with the responsibility of preserving eco system endowed with various plants and animal species which has inherited the country with enormous bio-diversity. The main function of the Department of Wildlife Conservation established in 1949 is the conservation of wildlife resources in Sri Lanka.

Yala

Without question Sri Lanka's most popular national park, Yala is situated about 260 km southeast of Colombo. Officially designated as a national park on 1st March 1938, Yala spans an impressive 978.807 km2. It's located along Sri Lanka's southern coast, overlapping Uva and the Southern Province, with Tissamaharama as the nearest town.

Sri Lankan Wildlife Introduction

Horton Plains
The Horton Plains is a unique grassland ecosystem found in the southern part of Sri Lanka. It is one of the last remaining extensive wet grasslands in the world. Home to numerous species of animals and birds, including the endemic Slender Loris and Blue Magpie, this location is a must-visit for wildlife enthusiasts.
Adam's Peak
Adam's Peak, the third-highest peak in the world, is located in the Southern Province of Sri Lanka. It is home to several endangered species such as the Blue-crowned Flying Fox, Yellow-vented Bulbul, and Sri Lankan Mongoose. With its picturesque landscape and diverse wildlife, this area offers an unforgettable adventure for nature lovers.
Central Highlands
The Central Highlands are home to several protected areas and offer a variety of habitats. This region is the habitat of several endangered species such as the Ceylon Flying Fox, Indian Rhinoceros, and Elephant.
